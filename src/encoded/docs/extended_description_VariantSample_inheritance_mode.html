Inheritance mode values are candidate labels based on novocaller, comhet caller, or GATK genotype assignments.

de novo calls:

  • strong candidate: Variants with novocaller posterior probability > 0.9.
  • medium candidate: Variants with 0.9 > novocaller posterior probability > 0.1.
  • weak candidate: Autosomal variants for which GATK genotype assignments are (Mother: 0/0, Father: 0/0, Child 0/1) and neither parent has 3 or more reads supporting alternative allele, but novocaller posterior probability > 0.1.
  • chrXY candidate: Variants on chrX or chrY, for which GATK genotype assignments are (Mother: 0/0, Father: 0/0, Child 0/1) and neither parent has 3 or more reads supporting alternative allele, These variants are not assigned novocaller posterior probability since since these chromosomes do not meet the model assumptions.
  • See Documentation for Details.

Variants of higher potential clinical relevance are assigned one of the following based on GATK assignments:

  • Dominant (Paternal): Father: 0/1, Mother: 0/0, Child 0/1.
  • Dominant (Maternal): Father: 0/0, Mother: 0/1, Child 0/1.
  • Recessive: Father: 0/1, Mother: 0/1, Child 1/1.
  • X-linked Recessive, X-linked Dominant (Maternal): Father: 0, Mother: 0/1, Son 1.
  • X-linked dominant (Paternal): Father: 1, Mother: 0/0, Son 1.
  • Y-linked dominant: Father: 1, Son 1.
  • Loss of Heterozygosity: Father: 0/1, Mother: 0/0, Child 1/1.
  • Loss of Heterozygosity: Father: 0/0, Mother: 0/1, Child 1/1.

Compound Heterozygous calls:

  • Two heterozygous variants are classified as Compound Heterozygous candidates if they are on the same gene.
  • Phasing: Two variants are classified as Phased if the pair of variants are confirmed to be coming from the two parents. (Mother: 0/1, Father: 0/0, Child 0/1) and (Mother: 0/0, Father: 0/1, Child 0/1). Other Compound Heterozygous candidates are classified as Unphased.
  • Variant severity: A variant is considered severe if it has a SpliceAI Delta Score >0.8 (S), or has a Clinvar interpretation of Pathogenic or Likely Pathogenic (C), or it has a HIGH or MODERATE Impact rating (Splice acceptor variant, Splice donor variant, Stop gained, Frameshift variant, Stop lost, Start lost, Transcript amplification, Inframe insertion, Inframe deletion, Missense, variant, Protein altering variant).
  • Pair strength: Compound Heterozygous pairs are classified based on the severity of the two variants (HIGH, MODERATE, S, or C).
    • strong pair: both variants are one of (HIGH, MODERATE, S, or C).
    • medium pair: one of the variants are one of (HIGH, MODERATE, S, or C).
    • weak pair: neither of the variants are (HIGH, MODERATE, S, or C).
  • See Documentation for Details.

Low relevance:

  • Variants on multiallelic sites (e.g, G, T, A in different individuals) are not assigned inheritance mode values, except possibly compound heterozygous.
  • Variants with missing calls (./.) or calls that mismatch the sex (0/1 on chrX for a male) are not assigned inheritance mode values.
  • Variants with genotypes that do not meet any of the high relevant criteria above are not assigned GATK-based labels.
